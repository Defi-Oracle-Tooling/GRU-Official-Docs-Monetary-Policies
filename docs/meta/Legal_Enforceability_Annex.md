# Legal enforceability annex — institutional claims checklist

> **Repository-wide status:** **Counsel-facing checklist — not legal advice.** Any claim elsewhere in this repository regarding **PIMI**, **treaty-level immunity**, **non-seizure**, **UNCITRAL / arbitration-first enforceability**, or similar **must** be read together with this annex. Until **filed instruments** and **jurisdiction schedules** exist, such language is **aspirational design**, not an assertion of enforceable rights.

## 1. Purpose

Charter and policy documents reference **UNCITRAL alignment**, **arbitration seats**, **PIMI-style** protections, **treaty-level immunity** for registered reserves, and **non-seizure** language. This annex does **not** provide legal advice. It lists **deterministic questions** external counsel and sovereign members must answer so claims are **operationally grounded**.

## 2. Question set (jurisdiction-agnostic)

| ID | Question | Why it matters |
|----|----------|----------------|
| L1 | **Which instrument** creates PIMI (or equivalent) status — treaty, host-state statute, contract, or trust deed? | Without a **pointing rule**, status is narrative only. |
| L2 | **Recognition:** which courts or regulators have **explicitly** acknowledged the framework? | Affects enforceability of immunities. |
| L3 | **Non-seizure:** is protection **in rem** on named vaults, **contractual** covenant, or **sovereign immunity** of an international org? | Each channel has different third-party effect. |
| L4 | **Mandatory local law override:** what happens if member state **AML**, **sanctions**, or **bankruptcy** law **conflicts** with non-seizure clauses? | Need **carve-out** matrix. |
| L5 | **Arbitration:** seat, applicable law, **ICSID / UNCITRAL** path, and **standing** (who may sue whom). | Determines remedy. |
| L6 | **Custodian enforcement:** how does a favorable award **attach** to a commercial vault operator in a third country? | Practical recovery. |
| L7 | **Member adoption:** what **votes** and **publication** steps bind a central bank or treasury? | Charter alignment. |

## 3. Deliverable

A **jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction schedule** (table per country: governing law, immunities, carve-outs, custodian chain) maintained outside this repo or in `docs/legal/` when cleared for publication.

## 4. Cross-reference

MiCA / IFRS treatment of asset-referenced tokens: compliance memos. **DBIS charter** remains the high-level institutional narrative; this annex forces **bridge** from narrative to **filed instruments**.
